Why this exists

Processor go-to-market engineering teams have a deadline calendar and a math problem on the same page.

The deadline calendar: 15 July 2026 FCA DPC PS26/1 for UK card. 14 November 2026 SWIFT CBPR+ cutover. PSD3 enforcement begins. Tokenized correspondent rails are no longer a 5-year story — Basel III LCR haircuts make the build case today.

The math problem: card take-rate compression, FCA DPC's effect on routing, FX netting savings the treasury team has been asking for, real-time fraud at FedNow speed, what the LATAM corridor actually costs when PIX / SPEI / Bre-B are on the table. Every processor exec deck does this math from scratch every quarter.

The eight scenarios here are the curated path — economics first, then the migration surface, then the corridor, then fraud, then the mandate the agent runtime downstream will read. Live now: the four that mattered most to ship first. The next four close the loop.

For the acquirer reader The processor stack is where the showcase has its longest list of already-live scenarios (4 of 8). The remaining four extend the same surface — same tokens, same export format, same audit trail. The buyer inherits a migration-period deliverable already half-shipped.
