BSA · FATF · 6AMLD — enforcement clocks running

U.S. Bank Secrecy Act (31 USC §5311 ff.) remains the foundational AML regime; FinCEN's AML/CFT National Priorities set the 2026 examination focus. FATF Recommendation 16 (the Travel Rule) and the 6th AML Directive (EU 2018/1673) add personal liability for senior management. AMLA (EU) begins direct supervision of selected obliged entities from 2028 with a single rulebook (Regulation (EU) 2024/1624).

Sources: 31 USC §5311–§5336 · FinCEN AML Priorities (2021, updated 2026) · FATF R.16 · EU Reg. 2024/1624 (AMLR) · EU Dir. 2018/1673 (6AMLD)

Value at risk · BSA + OFAC + 6AMLD

Up to ~$356,579 per OFAC violation · $1M+/day criminal BSA · 6AMLD personal liability for directors

OFAC's IEEPA civil cap (inflation-adjusted, 2024) sits at $356,579 per violation or twice the transaction value, whichever is greater. Willful BSA programme failures expose institutions and named officers to criminal penalties up to $1M/day under 31 USC §5322(b). Under the 6th AML Directive, named senior managers can be personally liable, including custodial sentences. The mandate codifies policy in a form an agentic payment system can both follow and prove it followed — turning policy documents into machine-checkable controls.
