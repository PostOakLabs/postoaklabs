BaaS · Custody · FDIC pass-through

BaaS programmes do not hold deposits directly. Customer balances are held by a sponsor bank on a pass-through basis; some programmes additionally sweep into money-market mutual funds for higher yield (and lower regulatory-deposit treatment). FDIC pass-through insurance only applies when specific recordkeeping and disclosure requirements are met (12 CFR §330.5, §330.7). After the 2024 Synapse–Evolve receivership, the FDIC issued a December 2024 recordkeeping rule proposal that highlighted these expectations — that proposal was subsequently withdrawn in March 2025; the underlying 12 CFR §330 pass-through insurance requirements remain in effect.

Sources: 12 CFR §330 (FDIC pass-through) · FDIC Recordkeeping Rule NPR (proposed Dec 2024, withdrawn Mar 2025) · GENIUS Act 2025 (stablecoin yield bar) · SEC Rule 2a-7 (MMF)

Value at risk · programme termination & custody misrepresentation

Synapse-Evolve precedent: $85M+ end-user losses, programme termination cascade, FDIC enforcement on misstated insurance

Wallet-float economics evaporate the moment a sponsor-bank programme terminates. The 2024 Synapse–Evolve failure produced mid-eight-figure end-user losses, multi-quarter receivership, and a wave of FDIC enforcement orders against fintechs that misrepresented pass-through insurance coverage. Sponsor-bank programme agreements typically include 60–90 day termination clauses if allocation or recordkeeping rules are breached. Binding the float-allocation policy into an Policy Mandate the runtime cannot silently relax — and that auditors can read — is the audit-grade response.
