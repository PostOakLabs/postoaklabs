Instant-payment rails are irrevocable — once the money lands, the fraud loss is final. The decision must happen in the sub-second window between auth and send. This lab models a six-signal fraud score against an adjustable threshold and intervention budget, surfaces the resulting confusion matrix and expected dollar loss, and exports the chosen policy as an Policy Mandate the runtime enforces and the fraud-ops team can audit.

FedNow · RTP · SEPA Instant — irreversibility means real-time matters

FedNow (Federal Reserve, July 2023) and TCH RTP (Real-Time Payments, 2017; $10M cap since February 2025) are credit-push, irrevocable rails. Once accepted by the receiving institution, recall is at the receiver's discretion. SEPA Instant (EU; SCT Inst mandatory for euro-area banks from Jan 2025 under Regulation (EU) 2024/886) is the European equivalent. Reg E protections for unauthorised transfers cover ACH; for consumer-initiated push payments on RTP/FedNow, recovery depends on the receiving bank's cooperation — which is rarely guaranteed.

Sources: Federal Reserve FedNow service guide · TCH RTP rulebook (Feb 2025 — $10M cap) · EU 2024/886 (Instant Payments Regulation) · 12 CFR §1005 (Reg E)

Value at risk · irreversible-rail fraud losses

FTC 2024: $1.1B+ reported consumer losses to wire / instant-transfer fraud · RTP fraud-recovery rate < 30%

FTC consumer sentinel reports for 2024 surface $1.1B+ in self-reported losses to wire transfer and instant-payment fraud. The FBI IC3 Internet Crime Report puts business-email-compromise losses on RTP/wire at $2.9B+ for 2024. Because the rails are credit-push and irrevocable, recovery rates run below 30%. The mandate binds the chosen threshold, intervention budget and step-up-auth policy so the runtime can act inside the sub-200ms window without re-litigating the policy on every transaction.
