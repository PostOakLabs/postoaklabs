SWIFT CBPR+ · structured-address mandate DEADLINE 14 NOV 2026

SWIFT's Cross-Border Payments and Reporting Plus (CBPR+) framework migrated payments-and-reporting messages to ISO 20022 in March 2023. The coexistence period ended 22 November 2025 — MT103, MT202 / MT202COV, and the MT9xx cash-reporting series are retired for cross-border FIN traffic; ISO 20022 is now mandatory. The remaining open milestone is the structured-address mandate: from 14 November 2026, CBPR+ payment instructions carrying unstructured postal addresses will be rejected. Hybrid address (town + country minimum) is the compliance floor; fully structured is preferred. The 2024 Eurosystem T2 migration completed the EU TARGET2 → T2 transition on ISO 20022.

Sources: SWIFT CBPR+ Market Practice Guidelines · ISO 20022 Programme · Eurosystem T2 launch (Mar 2023) · Fed FedWire ISO 20022 migration (Mar 2025)

Value at risk · cutover non-readiness

Per-month rejection cost ~$35K + correspondent-relationship termination + reconciliation backlog measured in days

Since the 22 November 2025 MT retirement, inbound MT-format cross-border traffic on the FIN service is rejected. From 14 November 2026, messages with unstructured addresses face rejection on the CBPR+ path too. Each rejected payment carries a per-message investigation cost (~$35–$60 industry estimate), a customer-experience hit, and a multi-day reconciliation tail. Repeated rejections trigger correspondent-bank investigations and, in the worst case, correspondent-relationship termination — a far higher cost than the operational rejection itself. The mandate codifies the chosen message-family coverage so the programme team can prove status at every steering committee.
