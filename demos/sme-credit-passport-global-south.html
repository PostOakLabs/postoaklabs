Global South SME Lending Passport

A single board-ready readiness scorecard combining credit-risk, working-capital and revenue-based-finance economics — designed to be presented to local commercial banks in Brazil and Nigeria when sizing an SME facility.

IFC / BIS · SME framing

The scoring weights (DSCR · revenue · age · payment behaviour · sector · adverse) align with IFC SME Finance Forum definitions and BCBS guidance on SME credit risk (BCBS 207, 2011) for jurisdictions where audited financials are scarce. The working-capital-gap method (CCC = AR + Inventory − AP) follows IFC's working-capital diagnostic toolkit; the factoring + RBF blend mirrors instruments commonly offered by IFC-partner local commercial banks in Brazil and Nigeria.

Sources: IFC SME Finance Forum · BCBS 207 (2011) · IFC Working Capital Diagnostic · CBN Microfinance Policy Framework

Value at risk · Global South SME credit gap

$5.2T unmet SME credit demand (IFC 2024 est.)

The IFC estimates ~$5.2 trillion in unmet credit demand from formal MSMEs in developing countries — much of it blocked by a missing pre-bank readiness layer. A board-ready scorecard with auditable scoring (IFC / BCBS-aligned) accelerates local-bank underwriting and surfaces working-capital instruments (factoring / RBF) sized to actual cash-conversion-cycle exposure.
