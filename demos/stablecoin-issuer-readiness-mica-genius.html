MiCA Title III · GENIUS Act — the two clocks issuers run against

MiCA (Regulation (EU) 2023/1114) Title III governs asset-referenced tokens (ARTs) and Title IV e-money tokens (EMTs). Articles 35–42 set reserve composition, segregation, redemption-at-par-on-demand, and authorisation duties; Article 111 carries penalties up to €5M or 5% of annual turnover, whichever is higher. The US GENIUS Act (2024) establishes the federal payment-stablecoin regime, bars interest payments to holders on payment stablecoins, requires 1:1 reserves in cash and short-dated Treasuries, and gives the OCC / Federal Reserve a path to direct supervision.

Sources: EU 2023/1114 Arts. 35–42, 111 · GENIUS Act 2024 Title I · MiCA Level 2 RTS · ESMA / EBA guidelines on Title III

Value at risk · MiCA Article 111 + GENIUS Act bar

Up to €5M per breach (or 5% of turnover) under MiCA Title III; GENIUS Act bar on yield to payment-stablecoin holders

MiCA Articles 35–42 set reserve adequacy, redemption rights at par, and operational safeguards. The lab tests issuer parameters against the floor and surfaces what the prudential authority would flag at authorisation. Under GENIUS Act 2024, issuers paying yield to payment-stablecoin holders fall outside the payment-stablecoin regime — losing access to the federal supervisory path. The mandate codifies the chosen parameters so the runtime cannot silently drift away from them between audits.
